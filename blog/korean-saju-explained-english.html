Korean Saju Explained: The 1,000-Year Astrology That Goes Deeper Than Zodiac

KunStudio · K-Wisdom Series · 2026-05-03

If you've watched K-dramas long enough, you've heard a character mention 'saju' (사주) — usually before a marriage or major life decision. Saju is Korea's traditional four-pillar astrology system, refined over 1,000 years of Joseon and earlier dynasties. Unlike Western astrology that assigns one sign per year, Saju maps four pillars: the year, month, day, and hour of your birth. Each pillar combines a heavenly stem (천간) and earthly branch (지지), giving 60 × 60 × 60 × 60 = 12.9 million unique chart combinations.

The most important pillar is the Day pillar (일주). Its stem is your Day Master (일간) — the core of who you are. There are exactly 10 Day Masters: Yang Wood (갑/Jia), Yin Wood (을/Yi), Yang Fire (병/Bing), Yin Fire (정/Ding), Yang Earth (무/Wu), Yin Earth (기/Ji), Yang Metal (경/Geng), Yin Metal (신/Xin), Yang Water (임/Ren), Yin Water (계/Gui). Korean tradition treats each like an archetype: Yang Wood is the tall tree (visionary, slow to bend), Yin Water is the rain (gentle wisdom).

Beyond your Day Master, your chart's Five Elements balance (오행: 목 wood, 화 fire, 토 earth, 금 metal, 수 water) reveals your natural strengths and gaps. A balanced 2-2-1-2-1 chart suggests well-rounded life skills. Zero of one element = active development area. Four+ of one element = potential excess to harness or watch.

Korean Saju is more than prediction — it's a 1,000-year self-reflection framework. Many Koreans treat it like a richer alternative to MBTI, useful regardless of belief in metaphysics. Used wisely, Saju helps timing major decisions and understanding compatibility (궁합) with partners, which is why Korean families consult Saju masters before marriages.
