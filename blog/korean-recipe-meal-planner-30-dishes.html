30 Korean Recipes for Home Cooking: Meal Planner + Banchan Diversity Guide

Korean home cooking philosophy: 5+ banchan (side dishes) per meal. This isn't excess — it's diversity. Every Korean meal includes pickled, fermented, fresh, and cooked elements. Korean dietary research links this 'banchan diversity' to Korea's top-5 longevity ranking and lowest OECD obesity rates.

Our 30-dish guide covers Korean essentials: Bibimbap (mixed rice with vegetables and gochujang), Kimchi Jjigae (aged kimchi stew), Doenjang Jjigae (soybean paste stew), Bulgogi (marinated grilled beef), Galbi (short ribs), Samgyetang (chicken ginseng soup, summer health food), Tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), Japchae (glass noodles), Sundubu Jjigae (soft tofu stew).

Plus regional specialties and street food: Pajeon (green onion pancake), Mandu (Korean dumplings), Tteokguk (Lunar New Year rice cake soup), Yukgaejang (spicy beef soup), Jjimdak (braised chicken), Bingsoo (shaved ice dessert), Hotteok (sweet pancakes), Kimbap (seaweed rice rolls), and more.

The weekly meal planner template tracks banchan count per meal — aim for 5+. Korean household pantry essentials: gochujang, doenjang, kimchi (always in fridge), sesame oil, anchovy stock. With these basics + rice + 3-4 vegetables, you can cook most Korean dishes.

Korean meal philosophy isn't about specific superfoods. It's about diversity, fermentation, balance. Our PDF includes 30 recipes with English ingredient lists, weekly meal planner template, and banchan diversity tracker.
