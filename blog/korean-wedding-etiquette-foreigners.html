Korean Wedding Etiquette: A Foreigner's Complete Guide to 폐백, 축의금, and Saju Compatibility

KunStudio · K-Wisdom Series · 2026-05-03

Korean weddings blend Western ceremony format with deep traditional rituals that catch foreign guests off-guard. The 30-minute Western-style ceremony is just the surface — beneath it lies 상견례 (formal family meeting), 궁합 (Saju compatibility check), 폐백 (traditional bow ceremony), and 축의금 (cash gift envelope) etiquette that has rules going back centuries.

The 상견례 (sang-gyeon-rye) is the formal meeting between both families before engagement. Father of the bride sits at the most important seat. Order matters: who pours soju, who eats first, where the gifts are placed. Modern young Koreans skip this but families often insist. Bring quality gifts (Korean ginseng, hanwoo beef, premium hangwa) — never cash gifts initially.

Saju compatibility check (궁합): many Korean families consult Saju masters before approving the marriage. The framework — Day Master compatibility, 5-element balance — is taken seriously even by modern educated families. Even if you don't believe in metaphysics, expect this conversation. If 궁합 is incompatible, families may suggest delaying the wedding or performing ritual mitigation.

축의금 (chu-gi-geum) cash gift envelope: white envelope, your name on it, black ink (NOT red — red is for living person celebrations like birthdays). Standard amounts in 2026: 50,000 won (acquaintance), 100,000 (friends), 200,000+ (family/close). NEVER give 4 (사) digit amounts (40,000) — '4' associates with death. Hand the envelope at the registration desk on the way in.

The 폐백 (pye-baek) bow ceremony happens after the Western ceremony — the couple changes to hanbok and bows to the groom's family. Family throws jujubes and chestnuts; bride catches in her hanbok skirt for fertility blessing. Foreign spouses can fully participate — Korean families love the cultural respect.
