Why "AI strategy" usually fails at SMBs

Three patterns we see when audits go wrong:

  • Tool-first thinking. Buying ChatGPT Teams without mapping which workflows benefit. Result: $300/mo, 80% unused.
  • Big-bang projects. Trying to automate "the whole sales pipeline" in Q1. Result: 6 months, no shipping.
  • No baseline. Implementing AI without measuring what it replaced. Result: cannot prove ROI to your board.

The 7-day framework fixes all three.

Day 1: Workflow inventory (60 min)

List every recurring workflow in your business. Bias toward "boring": invoicing, customer support tickets, hiring screens, weekly reports, social media posts, vendor onboarding, contract review.

For each, note:

  • How many hours/week it consumes (across the team)
  • Average hourly cost of the people doing it
  • Frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • Tools currently used

Most SMBs find 30–50 workflows in this exercise. That's normal.

Day 2: Score by AI replaceability (30 min)

For each workflow, score 1–5 on:

Dimension1 (low)5 (high)
RepetitivenessBespoke, judgment-heavySame template every time
Text/data heavyPhysical, hands-onAll in docs/email/spreadsheets
Tolerance for 90% accuracyMission-critical, zero errorHuman review catches issues
Volume1-2 per week10+ per day

Total score above 14 = strong automation candidate. Below 8 = leave alone for now.

Day 3-4: Tool match + ROI calc per workflow

For your top 10 candidates, match each to a 2026 tool category:

  • Email/text automation: Claude API + Zapier ($30-100/mo)
  • Customer support: Intercom Fin, Crisp Voice, Open AI Assistant API ($50-300/mo)
  • Document review: Claude with Files, Hebbia, Harvey for legal ($50-500/mo)
  • Meeting summaries: Otter, Granola, Fireflies ($10-30/seat)
  • Content generation: Claude/GPT + custom prompts (low cost, high prompt skill required)
  • Sales prospecting: Clay, Apollo + AI enrichment ($150-500/mo)

For each tool, calculate:

Hours saved/week × hourly cost × 50 weeks − annual tool cost = year-1 net savings

Real example: customer support automation saving 12 hours/week at $30/hr = $18,000 saved annually. Tool cost $150/mo = $1,800. Net year-1: $16,200. Payback in 30 days.

Day 5: Prioritize 3 quick wins

From your scored list, pick 3 with:

  • Year-1 net savings > $10,000
  • Payback < 60 days
  • Setup time < 2 weeks each

Skip "transformational AI initiatives" for now. Quick wins compound trust within your team. Big projects come in Quarter 2.

Day 6: Build the 30-day implementation plan

For each of the 3 quick wins:

  • Owner (1 named person on your team)
  • Week-by-week milestones (4 weeks max)
  • Budget approved upfront
  • Success metric (hours saved, tickets reduced, etc.)
  • Review checkpoint at Day 30

Day 7: Strategy call + sign-off

Final 60-minute internal meeting. Get all stakeholders on the implementation plan. Lock the 30-day commitment. Schedule the Day 30 review.
