## Question (find/post on Quora):
> Can foreigners get an accurate saju reading even if they weren't born in Korea?

## Answer (copy-paste):

**Absolutely yes — your birthplace has nothing to do with it.**

Saju (사주, also called Four Pillars of Destiny) is based entirely on your birth date and time, not your nationality or ethnicity. The system maps the year, month, day, and hour of your birth onto a grid of heavenly stems and earthly branches drawn from Chinese cosmology. It doesn't care whether you were born in Seoul, São Paulo, or Stockholm.

Here are a few things foreigners should know before getting a reading:

**1. Your exact birth time matters enormously.**
The fourth pillar — the hour pillar — can completely shift your reading. If you don't know your birth time, a good practitioner will work with approximations, but try to find your birth certificate beforehand. Hospital records often have the minute noted.

**2. Time zone and solar time are sometimes adjusted.**
Traditional Korean saju uses solar time (절기, jeolgi), not clock time. Some practitioners will convert your birth time to local solar time at your birthplace, while others use standard clock time. It's worth asking your reader which method they follow.

**3. The interpretation is universal, but cultural framing isn't.**
The symbols — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water, and the 12 animals — apply to everyone equally. What differs is how a Korean practitioner *explains* those symbols. They might use metaphors rooted in Korean family dynamics or Confucian social roles. Don't be thrown off; just ask them to rephrase if something feels culturally specific.

**4. Language barriers are very real, but manageable.**
Many younger saju readers in Korea now offer consultations in English, especially through online booking platforms. Overseas Korean communities in the US, Australia, and Canada also have practitioners comfortable with English-speaking clients. A bilingual reading is far better than running a Korean session through Google Translate in real time.

**5. Foreigners often find the reading surprisingly resonant.**
This is the part that gets people. Non-Koreans frequently report that a solid saju reading feels just as eerily accurate as it does for Koreans. The system is old enough and broad enough that human patterns of personality, timing, and relationships translate across cultures.

If you want to explore before booking a paid session, free online saju calculators let you generate your four pillars chart in seconds — great for learning your basic elements before sitting down with a practitioner. Korean culture ebooks and introductory guides to East Asian metaphysics can also give you useful background so you walk into the reading already knowing what questions to ask.
