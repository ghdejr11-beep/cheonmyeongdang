> How does Saju compatibility actually work for Korean couples?

## Answer (copy-paste):

**Saju compatibility (사주 궁합) is taken seriously in Korea** — even by couples who consider themselves modern and rational. Many families still consult a Saju reader before a wedding, and it's not unusual for young people to quietly check their partner's birth data before getting too emotionally invested.

Here's how it actually works:

**1. It's built on your Four Pillars**
Saju means "four pillars" — year, month, day, and hour of birth. Each pillar carries a heavenly stem and an earthly branch drawn from the traditional Chinese calendar system. Together they paint a picture of your elemental makeup: Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, or Water, in varying combinations.

**2. Compatibility is about balance, not sameness**
A common misconception is that matching elements are ideal. Actually, a skilled reader looks for *balance*. If one person is heavy in Fire energy, a partner with some Water can be stabilizing rather than conflicting. It's less about "you're a match" and more about "how do your energies interact?"

**3. The hour of birth matters more than most people realize**
Many Western astrology comparisons focus only on the year (your animal sign). But in Saju, the hour pillar is considered the most personal and intimate column. Couples who don't know each other's birth hour are working with incomplete information.

**4. Readers look for specific clashes and harmonies**
Certain earthly branches create natural harmony (삼합, *samhap*), while others form direct clashes (충, *chung*). A clash doesn't automatically mean disaster — context matters — but it does signal areas of friction that need awareness.

**5. It's often used as a conversation starter, not a verdict**
In practice, many Korean couples use Saju compatibility not to decide *whether* to be together, but to understand *where* they might struggle. A good reading gives language to dynamics the couple may already feel but can't name.

Personally, I've seen couples with "bad" compatibility readings thrive, and supposedly "perfect" pairs fall apart. Saju is a framework, not a fortune machine.
