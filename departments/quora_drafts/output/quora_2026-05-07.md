## Question (find/post on Quora):
> Are there free Korean Saju calculators available online?

## Answer (copy-paste):

Yes, there are quite a few free Korean Saju (사주) calculators available online, and they've gotten surprisingly good in recent years.

For those unfamiliar, **Saju** (also called the Four Pillars of Destiny) is a traditional Korean fortune-telling system rooted in Chinese metaphysics. It maps your birth year, month, day, and hour onto a chart of heavenly stems and earthly branches — giving you eight characters (팔자, *paljja*) that are believed to reveal your personality, life path, and destiny.

Here's what you should know before you dive in:

**What free calculators typically give you:**
- Your four pillars displayed as Chinese characters with Korean or English labels
- Basic elemental balance (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) in your chart
- A general personality overview based on your dominant element
- Sometimes a 10-year luck cycle (*대운*, daewoon) timeline

**The limitation of free tools:**
The chart generation itself is fairly accurate — it's just math, essentially. But the *interpretation* is where things get complex. A seasoned Saju reader (*사주풀이*) spends years learning how the pillars interact, conflict, and support each other. Free tools give you the skeleton; a real reading gives you the meaning.

**A practical tip:**
When using any Saju calculator, you'll need your **exact birth hour** if possible. In Korean tradition, the hour pillar (*시주*) matters a lot for personality nuance. If you don't know your birth time, most tools let you calculate without it — your reading will just be less detailed.

**Why Koreans still use these tools:**
Even younger, skeptical Koreans casually check their Saju for fun — especially around major life decisions like marriage, career changes, or starting a business. It's culturally embedded in a way that's hard to explain unless you grew up here. Think of it less like superstition and more like a personality framework, similar to how Westerners might read their horoscope.

**One cultural note:**
Korean Saju and Chinese BaZi share the same foundational system, so some Chinese-language calculators work perfectly well for Korean Saju too — just be aware the interpretive traditions have subtle differences.

If you want to explore further, search for **free online Saju calculators** (many Korean portal sites host them in English now), or look for **Korean culture and divination ebooks** that walk through how to read a basic chart yourself. Starting with a visual guide to the five elements is usually the best entry point for beginners.
