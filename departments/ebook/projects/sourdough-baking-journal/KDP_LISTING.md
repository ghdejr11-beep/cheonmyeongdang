## Description
**Bake Better Bread by Writing It Down**

Great sourdough isn't luck — it's a record. The bake that came out perfect last Tuesday? You can repeat it, but only if you wrote down the hydration, the dough temp, the bulk time, and what the crumb looked like. This **Sourdough Baking Journal** is built to capture every detail that matters, so your starter rewards you with consistently better loaves.

**Inside Each of the 100 Bake Log Pages:**
- Recipe & ingredients table (flour blend, weights, baker's percentages, water, starter, salt)
- Conditions block (total hydration %, starter hydration %, levain ratio, room/dough/oven temp)
- Full bake timeline (Levain Build, Mix/Fold, Bulk Ferment, Cold Proof, Bake) with start/end/duration
- Result & crumb scorecard (crust, crumb, rise, taste)
- Star rating + "will I bake again?" decision

**Also Includes:**
- Welcome & how-to-use guide written for real home bakers
- My Baker Profile page (starter name, birthdate, oven, goal loaf)
- Quick tips section — float test, dough temp, scoring, internal bread temp
- **Baker's Percentage Cheat Sheet** for 10 loaf styles (Country, Classic, Open-Crumb, High-Hydration, Whole Wheat, Rye, Focaccia, Sandwich, Baguette, Pizza)
- **Starter Maintenance Log** — 6 tracker pages, 108 feed slots
- **My Recipe Library** — 8 pages, 16 saved recipe cards for your winners
- 119 total pages — enough to take you from your first loaf to your hundredth

**Why Bakers Love a Journal:**
After ten loaves, patterns emerge. After fifty, you bake on instinct. The bakers in r/sourdough, in pro bakeries, and on every serious bread blog all keep notes — because flour shifts with the season, your kitchen swings 10°F between months, and your starter changes character after every refresh. Without records, you're starting over each weekend. With this journal, you're building a craft.

- Home cooks moving from no-knead to artisan loaves
- Bread-baking class students
- Holiday gift for the friend who won't stop talking about hydration percentages
