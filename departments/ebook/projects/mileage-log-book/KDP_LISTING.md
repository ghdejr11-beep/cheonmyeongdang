## Description (English, ~1900 chars)

**Track Every Mile. Maximize Every Deduction.**

If you drive for work, this Mileage Log Book is the simplest way to keep an IRS-compliant record of your business miles — and turn them into one of the biggest tax deductions you can claim. At the 2026 standard mileage rate, every 1,000 business miles can mean hundreds of dollars back at tax time. But only if you have the records to prove it.

This 105-page logbook is built for drivers who need clean, organized, contemporaneous records — without complicated apps, monthly subscriptions, or technology that fails when you need it most.

**Perfect for:**
- Self-employed professionals filing Schedule C
- Rideshare drivers (Uber, Lyft, Bolt)
- Delivery drivers (DoorDash, Uber Eats, Instacart, Grubhub, Amazon Flex)
- Real estate agents, realtors, and brokers
- Small business owners and freelancers
- Sales reps and field service technicians
- Independent contractors and consultants
- Caregivers and home health aides
- Anyone who claims a vehicle deduction on their tax return

**What's Inside:**
- Vehicle & owner information page (make, model, VIN, plate, insurance)
- Annual mileage summary with built-in tax deduction calculator
- 12 monthly summary pages with starting/ending odometer, business vs. personal miles, fuel and maintenance totals
- 84 detailed trip log pages — over 1,800 trip entries with Date, Start Odometer, End Odometer, Total Miles, Destination, Business Purpose, and Business / Personal flag
- 4 vehicle expense log sections for fuel, maintenance, tolls, parking, insurance, and repairs
- Year-end tax checklist and IRS resource list (Pub 463, Schedule C, Form 2106)
- Clear instructions explaining what counts as business mileage

**Why This Logbook Works:**
- Large 8.5 x 11 size — write big, write fast, no squinting
- Glove-box friendly and built to last a full tax year
- IRS-aligned record keeping (date, miles, destination, purpose)
- No batteries, no apps, no privacy worries
- Stands up in an audit — handwritten contemporaneous logs are still the gold standard

Stop guessing your mileage. Stop losing deductions. Grab this logbook, drop it in your glove box, and start tracking the miles that put money back in your pocket.
