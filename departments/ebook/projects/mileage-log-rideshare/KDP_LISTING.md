## Description (4,000 char)
**Stop leaving thousands of dollars on the table at tax time.**

If you drive for Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Instacart, Amazon Flex, or you're a self-employed contractor, this mileage log book is the documentation tool the IRS expects you to keep. Rideshare apps only show your **on-trip miles** — but the IRS allows you to deduct **all business-related driving**, including the miles between rides, the drive to your first pickup, parking searches, and the trip home from your last drop-off. With the 2026 standard mileage rate, every untracked mile costs you real dollars in your refund.

This 6"×9" paperback gives you everything you need to capture every deductible mile, contemporaneously, the way the IRS Publication 463 actually requires.

### What's inside
- **Vehicle Profile page** — VIN, plate, odometer, insurance, and platform info in one place
- **IRS Standard Mileage Rate reference** with a 7-year rate table you fill in yourself
- **Annual Deduction Worksheet** — multiply business / medical / charity miles by the published rate
- **96 daily log pages** (8 per month × 12 months) — Date, Start Odometer, End Odometer, Miles, Purpose / Destination, Category column
- **12 Monthly Summary pages** — total miles, fuel cost, maintenance, tolls, parking, and observations
- **Annual Summary table** — month-by-month rollup of business / medical / charity / total miles
- **Tax Filing Checklist** — final review before you submit Schedule C or Schedule A

### Built for real drivers
- 6×9 inches — fits in a glove box, center console, or door pocket
- 115 pages on white paper with a navy & gold matte cover
- Clean, no-distraction tables that work at red lights
- Category codes (B / M / C / P) so you can mark a trip in two seconds
- Page totals at the bottom of every spread — no calculator needed at year-end
- Generous writing space, even with bigger handwriting

### Who this is for
- Uber, Lyft, Bolt, and other rideshare drivers
- DoorDash, Grubhub, Uber Eats, Instacart, Amazon Flex couriers
- Real estate agents, sales reps, and traveling consultants
- Independent contractors filing Schedule C
- Anyone deducting medical, charity, or moving miles on Schedule A

### Why a paper log still wins in 2026
Apps log miles automatically — until your phone dies, the app crashes, the cloud backup fails, or you switch devices and lose six months of data. The IRS auditor doesn't care about a corrupted database. A handwritten, contemporaneous log is the gold standard the IRS specifically calls out, and a paper book always works.

**Buy this log on January 1. Fill it in every drive. In April you'll thank yourself.**
