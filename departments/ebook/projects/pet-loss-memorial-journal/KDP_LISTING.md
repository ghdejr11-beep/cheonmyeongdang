## Description (4000 char max)

**For the empty bowl. The silent house. The leash you can't put away yet.**

When you lose a pet, the world is louder for everyone else and quieter for you. This guided memorial journal sits beside you through the rawest days, then walks with you across the long arc of healing - while making a permanent, beautiful place for who they were.

This is not a "12-step" book. There is no schedule, no judgement, no platitudes. There is only space designed by someone who knows what 2am of pet grief feels like.

**INSIDE THIS JOURNAL**

**THEIR STORY** (memorial pages, fillable)
- About my best friend - name, breed, markings, the look in their eyes
- How we found each other
- Who they were - quirks, sounds, things they loved and hated
- Photo memorial pages with prompts ("their best day", "just their eyes")

**FIRST WEEK PAGES** (7 longer entries)
- The first day without them
- Their empty space at home
- How my body is taking it
- The story of our last day
- Who has shown up - and who hasn't
- Their voice in my head
- Closing the first week

**60 DAILY GRIEF CHECK-INS** (3-5 minutes each)
- Heaviness scale (1-10)
- One word for today
- "I missed them most when..."
- Something that helped today
- A memory I want to keep
- One small thing for tomorrow

**WEEKLY REFLECTIONS** every 10 days to surface progress you can't feel.

**MILESTONE PAGES** at One Month, Three Months, Six Months, and One Year - the longer reflections that honor where you are.

**LETTER PAGES** - direct space to write to them. As many times as you need to.

**MEMORIAL LISTS** - "Ten memories I never want to lose" + "Their daily rituals" - protect the details from time.

**THE STAGES OF PET GRIEF** - a brief, compassionate explanation of denial, guilt, anger, sadness, and meaning - so you know none of what you're feeling is wrong.

**SUPPORT RESOURCES** - ASPCA Pet Loss Hotline, Cornell Pet Loss Line, Lap of Love, 988 Crisis Line, and trusted books on pet grief.

**WHO THIS IS FOR**
- Anyone who recently lost a dog, cat, rabbit, or other companion animal
- Children and teens grieving a family pet (the prompts are gentle enough)
- Sympathy gift for a friend or family member after a pet's passing
- Those still grieving years later who need a place to put it

**FORMAT**
- 6 x 9 inches - fits in a nightstand drawer
- ~95 pages, soft cover, print-on-demand
- Photo placeholder boxes - tape or paste your own pictures inside

**A NOTE ON LOVE**
The grief is the size of the love. You are not "too sensitive" or "too attached." You are a human who loved an animal completely - and that is the highest thing a heart can do.

**You don't have to be ready. You only have to open the page.**
