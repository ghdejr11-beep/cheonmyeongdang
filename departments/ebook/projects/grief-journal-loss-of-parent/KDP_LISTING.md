## Title
Grief Journal for the Loss of a Parent

## Subtitle
A Guided Companion for the First Year After Losing Your Mother or Father - First Week, 60 Daily Check-Ins, Weekly Reflections, and Milestone Pages at 1, 3, 6, and 12 Months

## Author
Deokgu Studio

## Description (4000 char max)

**The world is louder for everyone else now. Quieter for you.**

Losing a parent is the loss most people will live through and the loss most people are most alone in. The cards stop arriving. The casserole dishes go home. And then a Tuesday in February arrives and you cannot make it to the front door.

This guided journal sits with you on those Tuesdays. It is not a 12-step program. It is not a "moving on" playbook. It is a place - structured by someone who knows that grief is not a problem to be solved.

**INSIDE THIS JOURNAL**

**THEIR STORY** - memorial pages
- About my mother / father - name, voice, hands, laugh
- Who they were - what they loved, their faith, their hardest year
- Two photo placeholder pages with prompts ("when I look at this photo...")

**FIRST WEEK** - seven longer entries for the rawest days
- Day One - the first day without them
- Day Two - the people who showed up (and who didn't)
- Day Three - their empty place
- Day Four - the story of their last day
- Day Five - how my body is taking it
- Day Six - their voice in my head
- Day Seven - closing the first week

**60 DAILY CHECK-INS** - a 5-minute rhythm for the hardest two months
- Heaviness scale (1-10) - one word for today
- "I missed them most when..."
- Something that helped (or did not)
- One small thing for tomorrow

**WEEKLY REFLECTIONS** every ~10 days - to surface progress you cannot feel.

**MILESTONE PAGES** at 1 Month, 3 Months, 6 Months, and 1 Year - the longer reflections that honor where you are. Letters to them, letters to your Day-One self, the "firsts" you have carried.

**FIVE LETTER PAGES** - direct space to write to them. Dear ____________________.

**MEMORIAL LISTS**
- Ten Things They Taught Me
- Ten Memories I Never Want to Lose
- Their Sayings, Their Phrases
- Five Traits I Hope I Inherited

**THE STAGES OF GRIEF** - including David Kessler's sixth stage, *Meaning* - so you know nothing you feel is wrong.

**SUPPORT RESOURCES** - 988 Lifeline, SAMHSA, GriefShare, Modern Loss, The Dinner Party, and the books that have helped many readers (Megan Devine, Joan Didion, Hope Edelman).

**WHO THIS IS FOR**
- Adults grieving the recent death of a mother or father
- Adult children whose parent died years ago and the grief has resurfaced
- Sympathy gift for a friend, sibling, or coworker after a parent's passing
- Those navigating anticipatory grief during a parent's terminal illness

**FORMAT**
- 6 x 9 in - fits in a nightstand drawer or a handbag
- ~67 pages, soft cover, print-on-demand
- Photo placeholder boxes - tape or paste your own pictures inside
- Margins designed for left- and right-handed writers

**A NOTE ON LOVE**
The grief is the size of the love. It does not shrink. You learn to grow around it - and one day you find you have built a larger life because of who they were.

**You don't have to be ready. You only have to open the page.**
