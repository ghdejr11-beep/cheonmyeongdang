## Answer (342 words)

The English-language Saju learning ecosystem has improved dramatically in the last 2–3 years. Here's a practical roadmap from someone who started with zero Korean and is now reading charts comfortably.

**Phase 1 — Get your own chart (week 1)**

Don't start with theory. Start with your own chart.

Run yourself through a free English Saju calculator (Cheonmyeongdang has a no-signup version at cheonmyeongdang.com that returns your Day Master + 8 characters + Ten Gods + current Daewoon). Memorize your Day Master. That single character anchors everything else you'll learn.

**Phase 2 — Learn the 5 elements (week 2)**

Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water — and how they generate / control each other. This is the cosmology underneath Saju, and 30 minutes of YouTube on Five-Element theory gives you 80% of what you need.

**Phase 3 — Learn the 10 Day Masters (weeks 3–4)**

Each of the 10 possible Day Masters has its own archetype:

- Yang Wood (갑) = tall tree, leader, vertical reach
- Yin Wood (을) = vine, adapter, lateral grower
- Yang Fire (병) = sun, illuminator, public warmth
- (etc.)

Learning all 10 takes about 2 weeks of casual reading. After this, you can do a basic personality read on anyone whose Day Master you know.

**Phase 4 — Ten Gods (weeks 5–8)**

The 10-role taxonomy that labels each non-Day-Master character. This is where Saju becomes really interesting. Wealth / Officer / Resource / Output / Peer — each has a specific behavioral signature when it's dominant in your chart.

**English-language resources I recommend**

- **YouTube:** Search "Korean Saju in English" — a few channels have appeared in 2024–2026 with structured beginner series
- **Books:** "The Korean Way of Reading Saju" (English translations exist of several Korean primers)
- **Apps with educational layers:** Cheonmyeongdang's reading explains each element of your chart inline, which is itself a form of guided learning

**Practice on charts you know**

Once you have basics down, run public-figure charts (just date is enough for 6 of 8 characters) and check if the read fits the public arc.
