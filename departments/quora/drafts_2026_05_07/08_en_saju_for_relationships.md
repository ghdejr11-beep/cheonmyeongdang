## Answer (304 words)

Yes — and the analysis is far deeper than "your zodiac signs match." Korean Saju compatibility (궁합, Gunghap) has been used for arranged-marriage matching for ~600 years, refined to surprisingly granular detail.

**What gets compared (16 characters total)**

Each person has **8 characters** (Year/Month/Day/Hour × Stem + Branch). Compatibility examines how **all 16 characters** interact across both charts.

**The 5 layers Saju practitioners check**

**1. Day Master harmony**
The Day Stem represents *you*. Two Day Stems can:
- **Combine harmoniously** (e.g., Yang Wood + Yang Earth → balanced)
- **Clash** (e.g., Yang Metal + Yang Wood → metal cuts wood, friction)
- **Match in element class** → similar values, also similar blind spots

**2. Five-element complementarity**
Healthy compatibility usually means **the elements you lack, your partner has**, and vice versa. Two people both maxed out in Fire (火) often burn each other out.

**3. Special Stars (Sinsal)**
- *Cheoneul Gwiin* (Noble Helper) → one partner becomes a karmic mentor
- *Dohwa* (Peach Blossom) → strong physical attraction
- *Yeokma* (Travel Horse) → couple thrives with movement, struggles when stationary
- *Baekho* (White Tiger) → emotional intensity, needs maturity

**4. Hidden conflict patterns**
Branch clashes (子午冲, 卯酉冲, etc.) signal "long-term recurring argument areas" — like one partner valuing freedom and the other security.

**5. Luck cycle alignment (대운)**
If both partners are in **rising 10-year cycles together**, the relationship feels effortless. If one is in a declining cycle while the other ascends, the partnership feels lopsided — not doomed, but it requires conscious patience.

**The honest takeaway**

Gunghap is **not a yes/no verdict** — modern Korean practitioners use it as **early warning + communication map**. Couples with "bad" Gunghap routinely succeed if they understand their friction points in advance.
