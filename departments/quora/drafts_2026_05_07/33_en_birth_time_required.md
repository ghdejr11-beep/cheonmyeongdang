## Answer (310 words)

Short answer: birth time is **strongly preferred** but **not required**. Saju still works without it; you just lose one of the four pillars and the late-life / hidden-self granularity that pillar provides.

**With birth time → 8 characters (4 pillars)**

- Year Pillar
- Month Pillar
- Day Pillar
- Hour Pillar (the one you lose without time)

**Without birth time → 6 characters (3 pillars)**

You still have:

- Year Pillar (ancestral context, early life)
- Month Pillar (career, social environment)
- Day Pillar (you and your spouse — including your **Day Master**, which is the most important character in the entire chart)

**What you lose without the Hour Pillar**

- **Late-life patterns** — the Hour Pillar traditionally reads as the late-life and end-of-life layer
- **Children-and-output reading** — the Hour Pillar carries information about output, expression, and children
- **Hidden-self layer** — the Hour Pillar's branch contains "hidden stems" that reveal subconscious patterns

**What you DON'T lose**

- Your Day Master (the single most important character) — fully preserved
- Your dominant Five-Element distribution (most of it)
- Your dominant Ten Gods (most of them)
- Your Daewoon (10-year cycles) — fully calculable from date alone
- Your year-by-year fortune (Saewoon) — fully calculable

**Practical recommendation**

If you don't know your birth time, run the 6-character reading anyway. It's 75% of the value of an 8-character reading. The remaining 25% is meaningful but not blocking.

**For the time itself**

If you can find it later (birth certificate, parent's memory, hospital records), you can re-run the chart and get the additional layers. But don't let "no birth time" stop you from starting.

**Try a 6-character or 8-character read free:** Cheonmyeongdang accepts both — the engine handles birth-time-unknown gracefully.
