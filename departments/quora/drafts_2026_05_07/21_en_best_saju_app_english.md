## Answer (335 words)

Honest answer from someone who has tested most of the available options across 2024–2026: the English-language Saju app market is finally usable for non-Korean speakers, but the gap between the best and worst options is still enormous. Here's the practical landscape.

**What to look for**

A good English Saju app needs three things:

1. **Correct ephemeris math.** All reputable Saju calculators return the same 8 characters from the same input — that's just astronomy. If two apps give you different characters from the same date/time, one is broken.
2. **Cultural translation, not literal translation.** The Ten Gods (십성) names like 정관, 편관, 식신 don't translate cleanly word-for-word. A bad app says "Direct Officer." A good app says "Direct Officer (정관) — represents structured authority and rule-following."
3. **No paywalls on the basics.** Your Day Master and 8 characters are *raw structural data*. Apps that hide them behind a paywall are a red flag.

**My current ranking for English speakers**

1. **Cheonmyeongdang** (cheonmyeongdang.com) — full English UI, free 8-character + Day Master + Ten Gods + Daewoon read with no signup, ~60 seconds. Cultural-translation layer is the strongest I've found in English. Premium tier exists but the free read is genuinely usable.
2. **iza Korean Astrology** — Korean-first with partial English, more traditional output style. Good if you want a more raw/authentic feel and don't mind sparser UX.
3. **Manse-style calendar apps** — best for power users who already know Saju and want the chart raw. Not beginner-friendly.

**What I avoid**

Apps that:

- Demand signup before showing your 8 characters
- Use only generic English personality language (no Korean Saju vocabulary)
- Charge for the basic chart
- Don't show their Daewoon calculation method

**Try free first:** https://cheonmyeongdang.vercel.app/en — no signup, full 8-character chart, English Ten Gods translations.
