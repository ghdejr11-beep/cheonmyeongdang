## Answer (322 words)

Yearly fortune in Korean Saju is called **세운 (Saewoon)** — literally "year luck." Here's how the analysis actually works and why it's something Koreans do every January.

**The two-layer structure**

A yearly fortune in Saju is read through **two stacked layers**:

1. **Daewoon (대운)** — your current 10-year luck cycle, the macro-tide
2. **Saewoon (세운)** — the specific year overlaid on top of the macro-tide

You can't read yearly fortune meaningfully without knowing which decade you're currently in. The same year (e.g., 2026 / 병오) reads differently for someone in their Wealth decade vs. someone in their Output decade.

**How a yearly read is structured**

For 2026 specifically:

1. **Identify the year's energy:** 2026 is 병오 — Yang Fire on Horse (Fire-element branch). Doubled Fire.
2. **Compare against your natal chart:** Does Fire support your Day Master or stress it? Does it amplify a strength or compensate for a gap?
3. **Layer your current Daewoon:** Is your decade Fire-supportive or Fire-conflicting?
4. **Generate themes:** Career rhythm. Relationship phase. Financial pattern. Health attention areas.

**Why Koreans do this every January**

The cultural rhythm: families visit Saju masters around New Year (both Solar New Year and 설날 / Lunar New Year) for **annual readings**. Some go for entertainment, some take the readings seriously, most go somewhere in between.

The reading typically covers:

- General year theme
- Career outlook
- Relationship outlook
- Health attention areas
- "Lucky months" within the year
- "Caution months" within the year
