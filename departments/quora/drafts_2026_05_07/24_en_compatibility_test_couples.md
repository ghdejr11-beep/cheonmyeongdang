Yes — and it's one of the most culturally important applications of Saju in Korea. The system is called **궁합 (gunghap)**, literally "palace matching." Here's how it actually works.

**What 궁합 evaluates**

When two charts are compared (yours + your partner's), the system runs **16 characters total** (8 from each person) through several layers:

1. **Day Master compatibility.** Your Day Master vs theirs. Some pairings are structurally harmonious (e.g., Yin Wood + Yang Earth — Wood draws from Earth, polarity balances). Others are tension-prone (e.g., Yin Fire + Yang Water — fire-water clash without polarity moderation).

2. **Ten Gods overlap.** What roles dominate each chart and how they interact. The most diagnostic pattern is **"Officer × Output"** — when one partner's chart has strong Officer star (rule-keeper) and the other has strong Hurting Officer (rule-defier), the recurring conflict is predictable: "you're rigid" vs "you're disrespectful."

3. **Element complementarity.** Whether the two charts together cover the Five Elements or leave gaps. Two partners with the same elemental zero may struggle to support each other in that area.

4. **Daewoon synchronization.** Whether you're in compatible 10-year luck cycle phases. Two great charts can feel "off-rhythm" if one is in a Wealth decade (consolidation) while the other is in an Output decade (exploration).

**What 궁합 actually predicts well**

Recurring conflict patterns. Long-arc compatibility. Whether you're in compatible life-phases. Where each partner's blind spots are.

**What it doesn't predict**

Whether a specific relationship "works." Korean Saju masters are explicit that 궁합 is **diagnostic, not deterministic**. Two charts with mediocre compatibility can have wonderful marriages with awareness; two charts with great compatibility can fail through neglect.

**The cultural use**

In Korea, families historically consulted Saju masters before approving marriages. Modern usage is gentler — couples often run their 궁합 to understand their structural patterns and develop awareness language for recurring conflicts.
