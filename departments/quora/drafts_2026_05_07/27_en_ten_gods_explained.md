## Answer (340 words)

The **Ten Gods (십성, 十神)** are a 10-role taxonomy that labels each non-Day-Master character in your Saju chart based on its relationship to your Day Master. They're the layer that converts raw element analysis into actionable behavioral interpretation.

**The five pairs**

Each "god" comes in a pair (one yang-polarity, one yin-polarity version), giving you 10 total roles:

1. **Peer / Competitor (비견 / 겁재)** — same-element peers. How you relate to friends, siblings, and equals.
2. **Output / Hurting Officer (식신 / 상관)** — what you produce. Creative expression, communication, children, output.
3. **Wealth / Direct Wealth (정재 / 편재)** — what you accumulate. Money, possessions, romantic interests (for men), patience-rewarded gains.
4. **Officer / Direct Officer (정관 / 편관)** — what controls you. Authority, rules, structure, partners (for women), pressure, discipline.
5. **Resource / Indirect Resource (정인 / 편인)** — what supports you. Mother, education, home, knowledge, internal reservoirs.

**How to read your dominant Ten Gods**

When you run your chart, count how many characters fall into each role. The roles with 2+ characters are your **dominant Ten Gods** — and they describe your natural orientation.

Common patterns:

- **Strong Wealth + strong Output** = entrepreneur archetype, creates and accumulates
- **Strong Officer + strong Resource** = traditional professional, structure + study
- **Strong Output + weak Resource** = creative without much external support, self-taught
- **Strong Peer** = team-oriented, identity formed through groups
- **Strong Officer + weak Wealth** = high responsibility, low compensation patterns

**Why Ten Gods is more useful than Western houses**

Western astrology distributes life areas across 12 houses (career, relationships, money, etc.). Saju compresses similar information into 10 *roles* — which behave more like personality structures than topic areas. The Wealth role isn't just "your money topic" — it's *how you relate to accumulation*, which then expresses across career, relationships, and self-image.
