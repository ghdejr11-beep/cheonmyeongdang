## Answer (332 words)

The **Five Elements (오행, 五行)** — Wood (木), Fire (火), Earth (土), Metal (金), Water (水) — are the cosmological foundation underneath all of Korean Saju. Every character in your chart maps to one element, and the *distribution* of elements across your 8 characters is one of the most actionable insights Saju produces.

**The element archetypes**

- **Wood (木)** — Growth, expansion, ambition, ideas, vertical movement, spring
- **Fire (火)** — Expression, illumination, passion, public visibility, summer
- **Earth (土)** — Stability, accumulation, trust, foundation, late-summer
- **Metal (金)** — Refinement, decisiveness, discipline, judgment, autumn
- **Water (水)** — Intelligence, intuition, flow, depth, winter

**The two interaction cycles**

Elements interact through two cycles:

- **Generation cycle (생):** Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood. Each element generates the next.
- **Control cycle (극):** Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → Wood. Each element controls the next (Wood breaks Earth; Earth dams Water; Water extinguishes Fire; Fire melts Metal; Metal cuts Wood).

These two cycles are how Saju explains why two charts harmonize or conflict.

**How to use your element count**

Run your Saju chart and count how many of your 8 characters fall into each element. You'll typically see something like "3 Earth, 2 Metal, 2 Wood, 1 Fire, 0 Water."

The most actionable read is the **zero (or near-zero) element**. That's something your chart can't generate internally — you have to source it externally through environment, lifestyle, partners, or deliberate practice.

**Quick "missing element" prescriptions**

- **Missing Water** → live near water, drink more deliberately, journal, swim, wear black/dark blue
- **Missing Fire** → expressive activities, public performance, red colors, southern direction
- **Missing Wood** → green spaces, growth-oriented projects, eastern direction
- **Missing Earth** → community, stability practices, yellow/brown, central focus
- **Missing Metal** → discipline, refinement work, white/silver, western direction
