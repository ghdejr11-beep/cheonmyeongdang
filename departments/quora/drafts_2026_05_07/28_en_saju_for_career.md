## Answer (336 words)

Yes — and career-fitting is one of Saju's strongest applications. Here's how the analysis actually works.

**Three layers determine career fit**

1. **Day Master archetype.** The 1-of-10 archetype that anchors your chart. Each Day Master has natural roles. A Yang Earth Day Master (mountain) fits long-tenure leadership and community-anchoring roles. A Yin Wood Day Master (vine) fits relationship-driven, adaptive, connector roles. A Yang Metal Day Master (sword) fits decisive, surgical, executive work.

2. **Dominant Ten Gods.** The roles with the most characters in your chart. Strong **Output (식신/상관)** = creative, expressive, communication-driven careers (writer, designer, teacher, entertainer). Strong **Wealth (재성)** = entrepreneurial, transactional, sales-and-investment careers. Strong **Officer (관성)** = structured, hierarchy-respecting careers (legal, government, corporate ladder). Strong **Resource (인성)** = research, scholarship, healthcare, teaching, knowledge-stewardship roles.

3. **Daewoon (current 10-year decade).** Even with the right natural fit, the wrong decade can make a career flat. The Wealth decade activates accumulation careers; the Output decade activates expression careers; the Officer decade activates authority careers. Reading your current Daewoon tells you which type of work the next 10 years rewards.

**A practical example**

A Yin Metal Day Master with strong Output, weak Resource, currently in a Wealth decade reads as:

- **Natural fit:** Refined, aesthetic, quality-focused work (designer, finisher, brand custodian)
- **Output dominance:** Creative-output careers
- **Weak Resource:** Self-taught, mentor-scarce path
- **Current Wealth decade:** Decade favors monetizing the output

Practical recommendation: design / brand / finishing-craft work, with active monetization push during the current decade, while building self-sourced learning routines to compensate for weak Resource.

**What Saju doesn't tell you**

Specific job titles or companies. The system describes structural fit, not literal career names. The interpretation work — converting "Strong Output + weak Resource + Wealth decade" into "design/brand/freelance" — is yours.
