## Answer (320 words)

This is one of the most important distinctions in Korean Saju and one of the layers most free apps skip — because it requires interpretive judgment, not just lookups. Here's how it works.

**The distinction**

Two people with the *same* Day Master (e.g., both Yang Earth) live very differently if one has a **strong** Yang Earth and the other has a **weak** Yang Earth. The Day Master archetype is the same; the *strategy* for that archetype inverts.

**What determines strength**

Your Day Master's strength is determined by:

1. **Same-element support in the chart.** A Yang Earth chart with multiple other Earth characters is structurally strong. Earth has reinforcement.
2. **Generating-element support.** The element that *generates* your Day Master in the Five-Element cycle. For Yang Earth, that's Fire. Fire-rich charts strengthen Yang Earth.
3. **Controlling/draining elements.** The elements that *control* or *drain* your Day Master. For Yang Earth: Wood (controls Earth) and Metal (drains Earth as Earth generates Metal).
4. **Seasonal birth.** Whether you were born in your element's strong season. Yang Earth is strongest in late summer and the transitional months.

When same-element + generating-element support outweighs controlling + draining elements, your Day Master is **strong (신강)**. Otherwise, **weak (신약)**.

**Why the strategy inverts**

- **Strong Day Master** → too much of "you" — the chart needs **outlets**: Output (creativity), Wealth (accumulation), Officer (challenges). Favorable elements are the ones that *use up* your strength.
- **Weak Day Master** → not enough "you" — the chart needs **support**: Resource (input), Peer (allies), and same-element. Favorable elements are the ones that *replenish* your strength.

The same Yang Earth with the same Day Master archetype lives a completely different optimal life depending on which side they fall on.
