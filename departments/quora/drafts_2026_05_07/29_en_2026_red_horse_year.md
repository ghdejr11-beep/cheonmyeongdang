## Answer (322 words)

2026 is **병오년 (Byeongo)**, often translated as "Red Horse Year" or "Fire Horse Year." Here's what that actually means in Korean Saju and why it matters culturally.

**The two-character label**

Every year in Korean Saju carries a 2-character label combining one Heavenly Stem and one Earthly Branch:

- **병 (Byeong)** = Yang Fire = Red color, expansive, public-facing energy
- **오 (Oh)** = Horse = Movement, expansion, public visibility, and the Horse branch is itself Fire-element

So 병오 = **Yang Fire on top of Horse (Fire-element)** — two layers of Fire stacked. Energetically maxed-out.

**What this means thematically**

Korean Saju treats doubled-element years as cultural inflection points. The 병오 narrative:

- **High visibility year** — public-facing things accelerate
- **Movement year** — people change jobs, relationships, addresses more
- **Volatility year** — what was simmering breaks into the open
- **Creative output year** — Fire is the element of expression
- **Risk year** — Fire untempered can over-consume; burnout patterns elevated

The previous Red Horse year was **1966**. Older Koreans remember 1966 as a year of dramatic change, and Korean cultural memory of "Red Horse year = upheaval" runs strong enough that some older couples historically avoided marrying or having children in such years.
