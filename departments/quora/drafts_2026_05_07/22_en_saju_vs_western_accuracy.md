"Accuracy" is a tricky word for either system, but the honest comparison is more interesting than people think. Here's a practical answer from someone who has used both for years.

**Same source data, different lenses**

Both Western astrology and Korean Saju run on the **same input**: birth date, time, and place. So neither has an information advantage from the raw data. The "accuracy" difference comes from what the system *does* with that data and what aspects of life it's optimized to describe.

- **Short-term timing.** Western transit work (especially Mercury / Mars / Jupiter cycles) gives day-to-day specificity Saju doesn't natively offer.

- **Specific event timing.** Solar arc and progressed angles can pinpoint event windows tightly.

- **Decade-scale identity arcs.** Saju's Daewoon (10-year luck cycles) line up with major life chapters more reliably than Western progressions in my experience.

**The fair conclusion**

Different tools, different optimization. Western astrology is better for short-term timing and psychological work. Saju is better for life-arc framing and structural relationship analysis. The "more accurate" question is wrong — the right question is "which tool fits the question I'm asking."
