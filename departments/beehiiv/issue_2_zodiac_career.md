# Your Korean Zodiac Animal — What 12 Animals Say About Your Career

Welcome back. **Issue #2** is the one most readers ask for first, so we moved it up: a clean career map for all 12 Korean zodiac animals.

Before we start — a quick note. In Korean Saju, your zodiac animal is your **Year Branch** (년지). It's only **one of eight characters** in your full reading, so treat the archetype below as a *strong tendency*, not a destiny lock.

## How to Find Your Animal in 10 Seconds

The Korean lunar new year falls in late January or February. If you were born in January or early February, your animal might belong to the **previous year**. (When in doubt, our [free reader](https://cheonmyeongdang.com/saju?utm_source=beehiiv&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=zodiac-career) calculates this for you.)

- 2024 Dragon · 2023 Rabbit · 2022 Tiger · 2021 Ox · 2020 Rat
- 2019 Pig · 2018 Dog · 2017 Rooster · 2016 Monkey · 2015 Goat
- 2014 Horse · 2013 Snake

Subtract or add 12s for older birth years.

## The 12 Career Archetypes

### Rat (쥐) — **The Strategist**
Pattern recognition, resource allocation, late-night thinking. Strong in **finance, data, supply chain, growth ops**. Avoid roles that punish improvisation.

### Ox (소) — **The Builder**
Long horizons. Doesn't flinch at boring work that compounds. Strong in **engineering, manufacturing, infrastructure, real estate**. The Ox builds the company nobody notices until it's huge.

### Tiger (호랑이) — **The Founder**
Risk tolerance is high; obedience is low. Strong in **founding, sales leadership, creative direction, special forces-style ops**. Tigers thrive when they own the outcome.

### Rabbit (토끼) — **The Diplomat**
Reads rooms instinctively. Strong in **HR, partnerships, hospitality, design, mediation**. The Rabbit closes deals everyone else thought were dead.

### Dragon (용) — **The Visionary**
Wants the biggest possible canvas. Strong in **product visioning, brand, entertainment, founding moonshots**. Pairs well with an Ox cofounder.

### Snake (뱀) — **The Analyst**
Quiet, deep, surgical. Strong in **research, law, surgery, security, investigative journalism**. The Snake notices what 9 out of 10 people miss.

### Horse (말) — **The Performer**
Energy moves rooms. Strong in **sales, performance, sports, broadcast, frontline service**. Avoid roles where you're invisible for months.

### Goat (양) — **The Craftsman**
Aesthetic perfectionist. Strong in **design, fashion, F&B, fine art, brand storytelling**. The Goat makes mediocre things beautiful.

### Monkey (원숭이) — **The Hacker**
Plays with constraints for fun. Strong in **engineering, comedy, product, growth marketing, anything with a leaderboard**. Monkeys break and rebuild things faster than the org chart can keep up.

### Rooster (닭) — **The Operator**
Detail-driven, time-disciplined. Strong in **operations, project management, accounting, editing, regulatory work**. The Rooster is the reason your company actually ships.

### Dog (개) — **The Guardian**
Loyalty beats short-term gain. Strong in **medicine, education, public service, security, customer success**. The Dog will outlast three founders at the same company.

### Pig (돼지) — **The Connector**
Generous, social, abundant. Strong in **community, hospitality, philanthropy, food service, content creation**. The Pig builds networks that compound for life.

## What This Doesn't Tell You

Two important caveats:

**1. The other 7 characters override the year animal often.**
A Rat with strong **Fire Day Pillar** acts more like a Horse. A Tiger born in a **Water month** is way calmer than the archetype.

**2. Your "great fortune" cycle (대운) shifts every 10 years.**
A Tiger in a Wood-friendly decade is unstoppable in founding. The same Tiger in a hostile Metal decade should consolidate, not expand.

## Reader Question

**"I'm a Rabbit but I work in engineering. Did I pick wrong?"**

Probably not. The Rabbit archetype shows up in engineering as **the diplomat between product and infra**, the manager who unblocks teams, the staff engineer who never burns bridges. The archetype isn't your job title — it's the *shape of value you create.* A Rabbit engineer often becomes the most senior IC because every team wants to work with them.
