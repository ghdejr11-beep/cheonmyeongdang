# 10 Essential Saju Concepts Every Beginner Should Know

> *"You don't need to memorize the whole library. You need to know which 10 doors open the rest. Walk through these and Saju becomes legible."*

Welcome to **Korean Saju Weekly Issue #9**.

Today's issue is your **bookmarkable beginner's reference**. After 8 issues of going deep on individual topics, this one zooms out. Ten concepts. Each one a doorway. Together they cover ~80% of what an intermediate Saju practitioner uses on a daily basis.

If you forward only one issue to a friend who's curious about Saju, make it this one.

---

## 1. The 8 Characters (8자, Bāzì)

Your birth date + birth time + birth place produces **8 characters** — 4 Heavenly Stems and 4 Earthly Branches arranged in 4 Pillars (Year / Month / Day / Hour).

This is the raw material. Everything else is interpretation of these 8.

If birth time is unknown, you have 6 characters and the Hour Pillar is omitted. Saju still works — just with less granularity for late-life patterns and hidden self-layers.

---

## 2. Day Master (日干, 일간)

The single Heavenly Stem at the top of your Day Pillar. It represents *you*.

Every other character in your chart is interpreted **relative to** this one. Issue #8 went deep on all 10 Day Masters. If you only know one Saju concept, know your Day Master.

---

## 3. The Five Elements (오행, 五行)

Wood (木), Fire (火), Earth (土), Metal (金), Water (水).

Every character in your chart maps to one element. Counting your elements (e.g., "I have 3 Fire, 2 Wood, 0 Water") tells you your **elemental balance** — what you have abundance of, what you're starved of.

Zeroes matter most. A zero element is something your chart can't generate internally and must source externally.

---

## 4. The Ten Gods (십성, 十神)

A 10-role taxonomy that labels each non-Day-Master character based on its relationship to your Day Master.

The five pairs:

- **Peer / Competitor (비견/겁재)** — same-element peers
- **Output / Hurting Officer (식신/상관)** — what you produce
- **Wealth / Direct Wealth (정재/편재)** — what you accumulate
- **Officer / Direct Officer (정관/편관)** — what controls you
- **Resource / Indirect Resource (정인/편인)** — what supports you

Your dominant Ten Gods are the strongest signal of your natural roles, drives, and tensions.

---

## 5. Daewoon (대운, 大運) — The 10-Year Luck Cycles

Saju's macro-tide layer. Starting from a calculated age, your life is divided into 10-year periods, each with its own elemental and Ten-Gods flavor.

You're never just "a Yang Earth Day Master." You're "a Yang Earth Day Master currently in your Wealth decade" — and the latter half is what makes the read time-sensitive.

When Saju practitioners say "this is your year" or "this decade is your professional opening," they're reading the Daewoon layer.

---

## 6. Saewoon (세운, 歲運) — Annual Luck

The yearly layer that overlays Daewoon. While Daewoon is the slow tide, Saewoon is the wave on top.

Saewoon is what fortune-telling content focuses on (e.g., "your 2026 horoscope") because it's most actionable in the short term. But without the Daewoon underneath, it's just a snapshot without context.

---

## 7. Compatibility (궁합)

The 16-character cross-comparison between two charts. Used most famously for **marriage compatibility** in Korean culture, but also for business partners, roommates, and family relationships.

Compatibility starts with Day-Master-to-Day-Master comparison, then layers Ten Gods overlap, element complementarity, and Daewoon synchronization.

Most "we keep fighting about the same things" patterns are diagnosable from 궁합.

---

## 8. Sinsal (신살, 神煞) — Special Stars

The library of named patterns that appear in Korean Saju more than in modern Bazi. Examples:

- **Cheoneul Gwiin (천을귀인)** — Noble Helper. People show up to help.
- **Dohwa (도화)** — Peach Blossom. Charisma and attractiveness.
- **Yeokma (역마)** — Travel Horse. Movement and travel as theme.
- **Baekho (백호)** — White Tiger. Sudden change patterns.

Sinsal don't override the main read. They flavor it. A chart with Cheoneul Gwiin reads differently than the same chart without.

---

## 9. Strength Assessment (신강/신약)

Whether your Day Master is **structurally strong (신강) or weak (신약)** in your specific chart configuration.

This single distinction often inverts the strategy. A weak Yang Wood lives differently than a strong Yang Wood, even though both have the same Day Master.

This is the layer most free readings skip and most Premium readings include — because it requires interpretive judgment about supporting elements, not just lookups.

---

## 10. Favorable Elements (용신, 用神)

Once strength is assessed, you can identify the **elements that support your specific chart** — the favorable elements (용신).

These are personalized recommendations: which colors, directions, careers, life choices align with your specific elemental gap. A weak Day Master needs supportive Resource and Peer elements; a strong Day Master often needs Output and Wealth elements to release pressure.

용신 is the most actionable layer — it converts the analysis into life decisions.

---

## How to Use This List

The 10 concepts above stack in roughly the order shown. Beginners memorize 1–4 first (the structural basics), then layer 5–7 (the time and relationship layers), then graduate to 8–10 (the interpretive judgment layers).

You can read your own chart at the level of 1–4 with just a free reading. Levels 5–7 benefit from a Premium read or a practitioner. Levels 8–10 typically come from a deeper consultation.
