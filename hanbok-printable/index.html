📚 About Hanbok

Hanbok (한복) is the traditional Korean attire dating back to the Goguryeo era (37 BC–668 AD). Today it's worn at weddings, Lunar New Year (설날), Chuseok, Dol (1st birthday), and Hwagap (60th birthday).

Color symbolism: Red & blue represent Yin-Yang. Five primary colors (오방색) — white, black, blue, yellow, red — align with the Five Elements of Korean cosmology.

Children's hanbok features 색동 (rainbow stripe sleeves) symbolizing healthy growth and bright future.
